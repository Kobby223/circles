Establish expectations from day one.

Establish expectations from day one.

The phrase “nature abhors a vacuum” is a bit of a cliché, but it’s true. New employees and new team members tend to arrive as relatively blank slates—open to an array of company cultures—but they will quickly start seeking cues for how to operate as a member of your company.

02

Respect your team members as individuals.

Respect your team members as individuals.

At work, you want your employees to be part of a team, but you must also have perspective: these are individuals with stories of their own. They got this far in life without your company, and they presumably have rich and varied lives when they leave work each day. It is important to not regard new team members as bodies who will perform tasks.

03

Engender connections within the team

Engender connections within the team

While it’s critically important that you value and honor each individual member of the team, it’s also important that the team members themselves exhibit that same respect and care toward one another. Encourage individuals to not regard one another as a body who sits at the desk next to them, but as a business partner who will work toward a shared goal of business development, individual success, and achieving team goals.
